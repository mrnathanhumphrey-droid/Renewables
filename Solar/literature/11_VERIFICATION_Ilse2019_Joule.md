Verdict: VERIFIED.

The arid regions almost certainly exhibit higher annual losses than the global aggregate, but the specific "3-7%/yr in arid regions if uncleaned" wording was NOT confirmed verbatim. **Caveat: our landscape ledger may be conflating Ilse's GLOBAL 3-4% / 4-7% headline with a regional-arid figure. - **C4: "Temperate region soiling losses 0.5-3%/yr"** — **CAN'T-VERIFY-FROM-ACCESS-LEVEL.** No snippet retrieved a temperate-region annual range from this paper. Plausible based on field knowledge but not extracted. Could be a derived/secondary claim rather than a direct Ilse number.

## Notes / disagreements with our attribution

1. **C3 likely conflation:** the "3-7%/yr" attached in our landscape language to "arid regions if uncleaned" actually appears to match Ilse 2019's GLOBAL aggregate projection (3-4% current global production loss; 4-7% projected by 2023) — i.e. a global average, not a regional-arid figure. The arid-regions-only number is almost certainly higher than 7% if uncleaned (consistent with snippet remark that "the increase in crystalline PV cell efficiencies achieved over the past two decades (about 10% relative) will be eliminated by a few weeks of soiling in arid regions"). **Recommend tightening the ledger to distinguish "global aggregate (Ilse 2019) ≈ 3-4% (2018) projected 4-7% by 2023" from "arid-regions-only uncleaned annual loss" (citation needed, possibly Figgis or Micheli rather than Ilse).**
